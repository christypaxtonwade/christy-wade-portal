About

The woman
behind the mirror

I am a violinist, writer, astrologer, and mirror. I have been teaching children to hear themselves — through music, through presence, through the particular quality of attention that a Suzuki teacher offers — for thirty years. Now I carry that same gift into the larger work: helping women hear themselves.

I am the Director of NWA Suzuki School of Music in Bentonville, Arkansas, and a keynote author in Revolutionary Icons (Kiva Publishing, May 2026), where my chapter Sovereign Descent maps the arc of feminine transformation through deliberate underground work — the descent that does not destroy, but refines.

I read birth charts the way I read bodies: as texts, as transmissions, as precision maps of the frequency a soul arrived carrying. I work at the intersection of astrology, sound, and the kind of mirror work that shows a woman what she already knows but hasn't yet been able to see clearly.

I am Gemini Sun, Sagittarius rising. I do not sleep easily. The owl is one of my primary totems. I am married to Tyler — my truest mirror — and I am the great-great-granddaughter of Christina, a Scottish orphan who stowed away on a ship and crossed America without a ticket. An expert, it turns out, in going underground to get where she was meant to be.

If something in you recognized those words, you're already in the right place.
