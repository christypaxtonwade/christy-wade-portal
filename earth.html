The Root Work

Every woman's compression has a history. The signal didn't weaken on its own — it was shaped by the channels it moved through, generation by generation. Mothers who couldn't hold what their daughters carried. Grandmothers who compressed themselves to survive. Great-grandmothers whose sovereignty was legislated away.

The lineage work is not about blame. It is about reading the code. Understanding what was passed to you, what you're being asked to complete, and what becomes possible when a line of women finally receives what they were always carrying.

My own great-great-grandmother Christina stowed away on a ship as an orphan and crossed America without a ticket. She was an expert in going underground to get where she was meant to be. That frequency lives in me. It may live in you too — in a different shape, under a different name.
