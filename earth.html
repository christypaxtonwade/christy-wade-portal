The Root Work

Every woman's compression has a history. The signal didn't weaken on its own — it was shaped by the channels it moved through, generation by generation. Mothers who couldn't hold what their daughters carried. Grandmothers who compressed themselves to survive. Great-grandmothers whose sovereignty was legislated away.

The lineage work is not about blame. It is about reading the code. I map each ancestor across five layers and tag them to specific Fields and Subterrains in the Navigation Map — precision work that names what was passed to you, what you're being asked to complete, and what becomes possible when a line of women finally receives what they were always carrying.

My own great-great-grandmother Christina stowed away on a ship as an orphan and crossed America without a ticket. She was an expert in going underground to get where she was meant to be. That frequency lives in me. It may live in you too — in a different shape, under a different name.

The Lineage

This is Christina. She arrived in a new country with nothing but her own coherence and the willingness to be her own midwife. She did not wait for permission. She did not wait for the right channel. She went underground, crossed the threshold, and built something from the other side.

Every woman who has compressed herself to survive carries someone like Christina in her bones. The work is not just yours — it is the completion of what they began.
